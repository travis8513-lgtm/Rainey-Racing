Born and raised in West Tennessee, Travis Rainey's passion for racing started long before he ever climbed behind the wheel of a dirt kart. Growing up, he spent countless hours riding dirt bikes, four-wheelers, and go-karts, developing a love for speed and competition at an early age.

In the early 2000s, Travis's father, Ronnie Rainey Sr., built and raced a Mini Stock dirt car. The car stood out with its bright yellow paint, bold purple 10X numbers, and unmistakable presence on the track. That race car left a lasting impression on Travis and inspired the yellow-and-purple color scheme and the iconic 10X that he proudly carries on his own kart today.

Some of Travis's greatest childhood memories were made at the dirt track. Nearly every Saturday night was spent with his dad, whether they were racing or sitting together in the grandstands watching the action. Those nights taught him that racing isn't just about winning—it's about family, dedication, and the friendships built around the sport. They sparked a lifelong passion for motorsports that still fuels him every time he unloads his kart.

Today, the same father who inspired Travis's love for racing plays one of the most important roles in Rainey Racing. Ronnie Rainey Sr. serves as the team's mechanic, crew chief, and trusted advisor. Behind the scenes, he spends countless hours in the shop inspecting every nut and bolt, maintaining the engines, dialing in the chassis, and making sure both karts are race-ready for the next green flag. His years of racing experience, mechanical knowledge, and attention to detail are a huge part of the team's success. For Travis, having his dad beside him in the pits is more than an advantage—it's a reminder of where the journey began and what family racing is all about.

Travis proudly pilots the 10X kart for Rainey Racing in the Predator Box Stock class, racing alongside his brother Ronnie. Together, the Rainey family has built more than a race team—they've built a tradition founded on hard work, perseverance, sportsmanship, and a shared love for dirt track racing.

When he's not racing, Travis enjoys spending time with his wife and three daughters, camping, fishing, and making memories outdoors. His family is the foundation of everything he does, and they are the motivation behind every lap he races.

For Travis, racing has never been just a hobby—it's a way of life. From the little boy watching his father race under the Saturday night lights to chasing checkered flags in the same yellow-and-purple colors that started it all, every race is an opportunity to honor his family's legacy while building one of his own. As Rainey Racing continues to grow, Travis remains committed to representing his sponsors, supporters, and community with pride while pursuing victories and inspiring the next generation to fall in love with dirt track racing, just as he did.

At Rainey Racing, everything begins with God. Our faith in Jesus Christ is the foundation of this team and our family. Before the engines fire and before the green flag drops, we trust God's plan for our lives. Win or lose, we give Him all the glory, because every blessing and every opportunity comes from Him.

Family is the heartbeat of Rainey Racing. This isn't just about racing—it's about brothers standing shoulder to shoulder, working together, encouraging one another, and building memories that will last for generations.

Recently, God blessed our family with the opportunity to become guardians of our nephews. Being able to raise them, love them, and introduce them to the sport that has meant so much to our family is one of our greatest blessings. We hope they'll grow up learning the same values of faith, hard work, humility, and perseverance that racing has taught us.

My love for dirt kart racing started when I was just 10 years old. From that first race, I was hooked, and now at 45 years old, the passion burns just as strong as ever. Today, I'm proud to drive the No. 3 kart in the Open Class, representing not only our family but the values we believe in every time we take the track.

One of the greatest blessings of this journey is having my dad beside me as my mechanic. He's the man who first taught me how to race, and after all these years, we're still chasing checkered flags together. The lessons he taught me extend far beyond the racetrack—they've shaped the man, husband, and father I strive to be every day.

I'm a proud husband and father of four, and together with our nephews, our family continues to grow both at home and at the racetrack.

Rainey Racing isn't built on a big budget or a large operation. It's built on faith, family, hard work, and a lifelong love for dirt racing.

Thank you for supporting Rainey Racing. Whether you're cheering from the grandstands, praying for our safety, or following our journey online, we're grateful you're part of our racing family.

God First. Family Always. Brothers Together. Racing with Purpose.
